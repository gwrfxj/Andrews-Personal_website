About Me

I'm Andrew, an ECE student at UW and Electrical Engineering Intern at PNNL. My current work focuses on analyzing SensorFish autonomous sensor data - characterizing fish passage conditions through dams by measuring acceleration, pressure, and rotation to support environmental impact assessments for hydroelectric infrastructure. I conduct field testing, calibration, and acceptance testing of Mini SensorFish devices to ensure they're ready for dam site deployment, while developing Python/Tkinter programs to modernize legacy data acquisition systems.

But I'm not just about research and engineering - I'm also a proud member of the UW Boxing Team, where mental toughness matters as much as physical strength. You can also find me in the gym at dawn, playing volleyball at the beach, jet skiing, wakeboarding, tubing at Lake Sammamish, or maybe jumping 15,000 feet out of the sky.

As a valedictorian who graduated high school a year early, I've always pushed to accelerate my learning. I've visited cutting-edge labs observing neural network-powered laser cutting systems and autonomous sorting technologies. Combined with my previous PNNL research on LLMs and Logarithmic Number Systems, these experiences showed me that modern engineering requires seamlessly integrating mechanical systems, electrical components, and computational algorithms to solve real-world challenges.
